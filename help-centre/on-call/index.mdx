## Setting Up On-call

<Steps>
  <Step title="Define your teams">
    Identify which teams need on-call coverage and map out their responsibilities. Consider service ownership and expertise areas.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Create schedules">
    Set up rotating schedules for each team. Consider factors like:
    - Rotation frequency (daily, weekly, etc.)
    - Team size and availability
    - Timezone distribution
    - Fair distribution of on-call burden
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Configure escalation paths">
    Define how alerts should escalate if not acknowledged:
    - Primary on-call engineer
    - Secondary/backup engineer
    - Team lead or manager
    - Executive escalation for critical issues
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Connect alert sources">
    Integrate your monitoring and alerting tools to automatically route alerts to on-call responders.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Test and validate">
    Run test alerts to ensure notifications are working correctly and escalations happen as expected.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Monitor and optimize">
    Use on-call readiness insights to identify issues and continuously improve your on-call system.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Best Practices

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Fair Rotation" icon="arrows-rotate">
    Distribute on-call duties equitably across team members. Consider load balancing and avoid burnout.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Clear Handoffs" icon="handshake">
    Establish clear handoff procedures between shifts. Share context about ongoing issues and recent alerts.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Documented Runbooks" icon="book">
    Provide on-call engineers with comprehensive runbooks for common issues and escalation procedures.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Response Time SLAs" icon="stopwatch">
    Set clear expectations for acknowledgment and response times based on alert severity.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
A well-designed on-call system balances rapid incident response with engineer well-being. Regular reviews and adjustments help maintain this balance.
</Note>

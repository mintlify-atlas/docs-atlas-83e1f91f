## Incident Lifecycle

<Steps>
  <Step title="Declare the incident">
    Quickly create a new incident when an issue is detected. Provide initial details like severity, summary, and affected services.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Assemble the team">
    Assign roles and notify relevant team members. Use escalation paths to ensure the right people are engaged.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Investigate and respond">
    Work together to diagnose the issue, implement fixes, and monitor the situation. Use incident channels for coordination.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Communicate status">
    Keep stakeholders informed with regular updates. Use status pages and announcements to manage external communication.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Resolve and close">
    Once the issue is fixed and stable, mark the incident as resolved. Ensure all documentation is complete.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Learn and improve">
    Conduct post-incident reviews, create follow-ups, and document learnings to prevent future occurrences.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Best Practices

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Clear Communication" icon="comments">
    Maintain transparency and clarity in all incident communications. Use structured updates and avoid jargon.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Defined Roles" icon="user-group">
    Assign clear roles (Incident Lead, Communications Lead, etc.) to avoid confusion and ensure accountability.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Accurate Timelines" icon="timeline">
    Keep your incident timeline up-to-date. This becomes invaluable during post-incident analysis.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Follow Processes" icon="list-check">
    Use your defined workflows and decision flows to ensure consistent, reliable incident response.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Warning>
During an incident, speed matters but accuracy is crucial. Take time to verify information before communicating to stakeholders.
</Warning>
